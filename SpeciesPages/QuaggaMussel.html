Invasive Species Project - Quagga Mussels

Quagga mussels (Dreissena bugensis) are a cousin species of the Zebra mussel and are also invasive to the Great Lakes Region. Like the Zebra mussel, they are native to freshwaters in Russia and Ukraine but were introduced later in 1989 in a similar manner from transatlantic vessels. They are now also found in lakes in Nevada and California.

What makes these species great at invading is their rapid reproduction rate, their ability to attach to a variety of different surfaces, and they can disperse during any life stage actively or passively downstream.

Solutions

Unfortunately, Quagga Mussels are not edible to humans due to the accumulation of toxins, pollutants, and microorganism within the mussel's body as they filter water. The main form of eradication of this invasive species is to use a type of molluscide and the main form of prevention is to "clean, drain, and dry" boats before moving to another body of water.
