Zebra Mussels

Description:

Zebra mussels (Dreissena polymorpha) are invasive species about the size of a fingernail. They are native to freshwaters in Russia and Ukraine but were introduced to the Great Lakes Region in the 1980s from a transatlantic vessel. In a decade, they colonized the river basins of the Great Lakes and beyond in Mississippi, Tennessee, and Ohio.

What makes these species great at invading is their rapid reproduction rate, their ability to attach to a variety of different surfaces, and they can disperse during any life stage actively or passively downstream.
